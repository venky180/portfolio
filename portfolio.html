Project 1: Native Inequality Assessment

Objective/Goal: Conduct a comprehensive examination of Native inequality using Census data. Replicate and expand upon existing econometric analyses focusing on poverty rates, access to resources, and various metrics of health and well-being.

Methodology:

  • Data Acquisition and Preprocessing
  • Construction of IV and DV Measures
  • Regression Analyses (linear and logistic)
  • Path Analysis
  • Causal Analysis with Regression Discontinuity
  • Statistical Visualization (graphs and GIS maps)

Results and Accomplishments: The project aims to provide a comprehensive understanding of Native inequality, uncovering determinants of economic disparities, resource accessibility, and overall well-being. The analyses will inform actionable policy recommendations.

Policy Brief Integration: The findings and methodological approach will be integrated into concise policy briefs by the Institute for Policy Research at Northwestern.
